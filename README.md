## Scientific Journal Papers
Saedi et al. (2025) proposed robust countermeasures to mitigate adversarial attack transferability in networks, highlighting innovative defense strategies applicable across digital infrastructure [1]. Nowroozi et al. (2025) introduced federated learning enhancements through cluster-based strategies, effectively combating data poisoning attacks orchestrated via GANs [2]. Saedi et al. (2024) enhanced 5G handover protocols by introducing a mechanism to block rogue base station attacks, a major vulnerability in mobile network security [3]. In their deep learning work, Saedi et al. (2023) presented RBS-MLP, a pattern-based anomaly detection model for recognizing rogue base stations in 5G environments [4]. A synthetic signal strength generation method for simulating rogue base station scenarios in 5G vehicular networks was developed by Saedi et al. (2022), aiding future experimentation [5]. Nowroozi et al. (2025) proposed a novel GAN-based poisoning and detection approach named VagueGAN, addressing dual aspects of attack and defense in federated learning [6]. A foundational VANET security evaluation method was presented by Saedi and Shojaei (2014), focusing on safety message dissemination protocols [7].
